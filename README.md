# Smart Closet

Final project for the Building AI course

## Summary

AI that helps you decide what to wear for the day! 
By accessing your closet AI can generate a plethora of outfit options and combinations, from shoes to accessories.
It reduces the unnecessary stress and time spent deciding what to wear each morning and, just maybe, unlocking new avenues in your personal style. 

## Background

This AI solution would minimize time thinking about what to wear and what would look good. Also this would reduce decision fatige allowing people to focus on more important things (relationships, work, community). I admit that this issue may be subjective, and could be more relatable to creative people and/or people care about their appearance. 
This AI project has been inspired by tech CEOs (Mark Zuckerberg, Steve Jobs) fashion takes: wearing the same thing everyday to eliminate decision fatigue. This solution could help you save energy for vital decision while being stylish.

## How is it used?

* **User Registration**
User creates an account and submits information like: gender, clothing size, preferred style, color preferences
* **Wardrobe Digitalization**
User builds a digital version of their wardrobe by adding clothing items through several methods (scanning a barcode, uploading an image, or manual entry)
Clothing system would store: category, color, material, season, secondary colors, pattern
* **Clothing Classification**
AI analyzes every item and automatically classifies it into structured categories.
* **AI Recommendation Algorithm**
Model would be trained on color theory, fashion style principles, an existing outfit database, user preferences, user feedback
AI complete combination: Tops, Bottoms, Outerwear, Shoes, Accessories
AI outfit options would be ranked based on color compatibility, style consistency and user preferences
* **User Feedback**
User could mark options as Like, Dislike or Save Favourites or potentially mark clothing items as unavailable (e.g., in the laundry).

## Data sources and AI methods
Data will be collected directly from users through barcode scanning, image uploads, and manual clothing entry. External datasets will also be used to train the AI's outfit recommendation model.

## Challenges
* Does not guarantee perfect style recommendations or understand individual taste completely.
* Requires user data collection, raising privacy and data security concerns.
* Image recognition may incorrectly classify clothing items.
* May reinforce fashion biases from training data.
* Does not replace human creativity and personal style choices.
